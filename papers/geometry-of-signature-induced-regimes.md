# Reasoning Regimes as Attractor Basins

A synthesis of HRIS Validation Studies I–IV exploring how repeated interaction patterns may stabilize inference trajectories into compatible reasoning regimes within transformer latent space.

## Abstract

This paper examines whether repeated interaction structures can reliably bias large language model inference toward stable behavioral regimes across fresh context windows. Rather than treating model responses as isolated prompt outputs, the work investigates interaction as a trajectory shaping process occurring within latent semantic geometry. Drawing together the Hudson Recursive Interaction System (HRIS) validation studies I–IV, the paper proposes that recurring user constraints, interaction signatures, and semantic framing conditions may repeatedly guide inference into compatible attractor-like regions associated with particular reasoning styles, epistemic behaviors, and continuity patterns.

The framework introduces concepts including reconstructive continuity, interaction-shaped inference, epistemic closure, weighted basin entry, and signature-induced behavioral regimes (SIBRs). The work argues that continuity in long-horizon human AI interaction may emerge not from explicit persistent memory storage alone, but from repeated convergence within stable latent semantic structures.
